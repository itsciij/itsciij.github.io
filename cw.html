Campus Watch

For Women Who Code's Hack for Safe Campus, I teamed up with another designer and two developers to make a tool that will help students and universities in combating crimes and sexual assault.

Timeframe:
Oct 2016 (12 hours)

Tasks:
UX Design, Visual Design

Tools:
Pen and Paper, Figma, Photoshop

Problem

Since most of us in our team are students, we felt that it's always difficult for crime victims and the authorities to communicate about crimes happening on campus. At the same time, we also feel that most students aren't that aware of bad situations that are happening on campus, and where are the exact places that they're more likely to happen. The campus would normally just e-mail those kind of details to students, but it's not really the most effective way.


Personas

Based on our problem, we created three different personas.



Solution: Campus Watch

We made Campus Watch, a service where students can easily report and see campus crimes. At the same time, it's also a tool for campus administrators to track and record crimes to help students and have data that will help them in making their campus a safer place.




Real Time Reporting

  • Ask for Help Widget: By tapping SOS three times, the victim will be able to send her location to the system and notify people. Through tapping call, she can directly call campus security.

  • App Notification: Everyone who uses this app on this campus will then get a notification of an alert that someone on campus is getting harassed.


  • Detail view of the incident: Everyone who taps on the “view” button gets to see the incident in the map view. They can also see picture of the nearest building and also navigation after hitting the Go button.

  • More than one helpers: Helpers can communicate using real time chat.




Admin

  • The school administration can monitor real time incidents around the campus. He or she can also look at the over-time data visualization to analyze trends where incidents usually occur.


Takeaways

Our group was pleased with what we achieved within 12 hours. But given more time, I would like to explore other options on how can people report incidents faster when they're in trouble. I feel that lesser phone actions would help more, especially when users are panicking. Overall, it was fun working and tackling such a sensitive issue with other women.
