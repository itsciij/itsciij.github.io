Kiten Rescue Web Redesign

One of our projects for our web and interaction design class was to redesign a non-profit organization of our choice where our focus should lie heavily on visual design. My goal for this project was to improve the overall look and quality of the website to help attract and engage its visitors.

Timeframe:
May 2014 (2 Weeks)

Tasks:
Branding, Visual Design, Illustration

Tools:
Illustrator, Photoshop, Sketch

Before and After




Design Decisions

The problems I saw in the current landing page is that is has an unmemorable logo, weak branding, and an unclear structure. As I do the rebrand, here are some of the things I considered:

  • Target Audience: I wanted to appeal to families of both young and old ages, so I settled for warm neutral colors that evoke a warm and loving feeling. I used sans-serif fonts that give a friendly vibe.
  • Hierarchy: I created user stories and used them to create a structured layout that prioritizes their needs when visiting the website.


Landing Page Sections

  • Hero: A dramatic hero with a strong tag line to quickly attract attention.
  • Description: A quick overview of the non-profit for those who want to learn more about what they do.
  • Actions: The 3 most common actions visitors can do: donate, volunteer and adopt.







Takeaways

Even though it's only a 2 week work, I enjoyed this project since it challenged me to apply my design skills in improving a real non-profit website. It taught me to assess both visual and usability issues with the site, explore some of the best modern design practices at that time, do a lot of user-research and gain feedback from the class.

This project is nowhere near perfect, in my opinion. If given more time, I would have explored or try different layouts that will improve the website's usability. I would also explore other color choices that will add more contrast as this one is a little bit too netural and desaturated.
